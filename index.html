Budgeting Mastery

The 50/30/20 Rule

  • 50% Needs: Housing, food, utilities, transportation
  • 30% Wants: Entertainment, dining out, hobbies
  • 20% Savings: Emergency fund, investments, debt payoff
Pro Tip: Track every expense for 30 days to understand your spending patterns
üèº

Emergency Fund

Your Financial Safety Net

Build 3-6 months of expenses before aggressive investing. This protects you from:

  • Job loss or income reduction
  • Medical emergencies
  • Car or home repairs
  • Unexpected life events
Start Small: Even $1000 can prevent most financial emergencies

Debt Management

Strategic Payoff Methods

Avalanche Method: Pay highest interest rate first (saves most money)

Snowball Method: Pay smallest balance first (psychological wins)

  • Prioritize high-interest credit card debt
  • Consider debt consolidation if applicable
  • Negotiate lower interest rates
  • Avoid new debt while paying off existing

Savings Strategies

Make Saving Automatic

  • Pay Yourself First: Auto-transfer to savings on payday
  • High-Yield Savings: Earn 4-5% APY vs traditional 0.01%
  • Sinking Funds: Save for specific goals (vacation, car, etc.)
  • Round-Up Apps: Save spare change automatically
The Latte Factor: Small daily savings compound significantly over time

Tax Planning & Optimization

Legal strategies to minimize your tax burden and keep more of your hard-earned money

üè¶ Tax-Advantaged Accounts

Retirement Accounts

  • 401(k)/403(b): Pre-tax contributions, employer match, $23,000 limit (2024)
  • Traditional IRA: Tax deduction now, pay taxes in retirement
  • Roth IRA: After-tax contributions, tax-free growth and withdrawals
  • HSA: Triple tax advantage - deductible, grows tax-free, tax-free medical withdrawals

Other Tax-Smart Accounts

  • 529 Plans: Tax-free education savings
  • FSA: Pre-tax healthcare expenses

üìâ Capital Gains Strategies

Long-Term vs Short-Term

  • Hold > 1 year: 0%, 15%, or 20% tax rate
  • Hold < 1 year: Taxed as ordinary income (up to 37%)

Tax-Loss Harvesting

Offset gains by selling losing positions. Can deduct $3,000/year against ordinary income.

Watch Out: Wash-sale rule - can't rebuy same security within 30 days

üìù Common Deductions

Standard vs Itemized

  • Standard Deduction: $14,600 (single), $29,200 (married) for 2024
  • Itemized: Only if > standard deduction

Common Itemized Deductions

  • Mortgage interest
  • State and local taxes (SALT) - $10,000 cap
  • Charitable contributions
  • Medical expenses (if > 7.5% of AGI)

üíº Self-Employment Tax Tips

  • Quarterly Estimates: Pay taxes 4x/year to avoid penalties
  • Deduct Business Expenses: Home office, equipment, software
  • SEP IRA: Contribute up to 25% of net self-employment income
  • Self-Employment Tax: Additional 15.3% for Social Security/Medicare
Pro Tip: Consider S-Corp election if income > $60,000/year

Investing for Beginners

Wisdom from Benjamin Graham - The Intelligent Investor

üõ°Ô∏è Margin of Safety

Graham's Golden Rule: Only invest when the price is significantly below intrinsic value.

Buy stocks trading at 2/3 or less of their true worth. This protects you from mistakes in valuation and market downturns.

  • Calculate intrinsic value using earnings, assets, dividends
  • Wait for market pessimism to create opportunities
  • Never overpay, even for great companies

üéØ Mr. Market Concept

The Market is Your Servant, Not Your Master

Imagine Mr. Market as an emotional partner who offers to buy/sell stocks daily at varying prices based on his mood.

  • Euphoric Days: Prices too high - sell or hold
  • Depressed Days: Prices too low - buy aggressively
  • Key Lesson: Use price fluctuations to your advantage
  • Never let market volatility dictate your decisions

üìà Defensive vs Enterprising Investor

Know Your Investment Personality

Defensive Investor (Most People):

  • Wants adequate return with minimal effort
  • Strategy: 50/50 stocks/bonds, rebalance annually
  • Use low-cost index funds
  • Avoid market timing and stock picking

Enterprising Investor:

  • Willing to devote time to research
  • Seeks bargain opportunities
  • Must achieve better returns than market

Core Investment Principles for Beginners

1

Start Early, Invest Regularly

Time is Your Greatest Asset

  • $500/month from age 25-65 at 10% = $3.8 million
  • Same investment from 35-65 = $1.1 million
  • Every year you wait costs you exponentially
  • Use automatic investment plans
2

Index Funds First

Start with the Market, Then Get Fancy

  • S&P 500 Index: Owns 500 largest US companies
  • Total Stock Market: Owns entire US market
  • International Index: Global diversification
  • Low fees (0.03-0.15%) vs active funds (0.5-2%)
  • Outperforms 90% of professional managers long-term
3

Asset Allocation by Age

Balance Risk and Return

  • 20s-30s: 90% stocks, 10% bonds
  • 40s: 80% stocks, 20% bonds
  • 50s: 70% stocks, 30% bonds
  • 60s: 60% stocks, 40% bonds
  • Rule of Thumb: (120 - your age) = % in stocks
4

Diversification is Essential

Don't Put All Eggs in One Basket

  • Spread across different industries
  • Include domestic and international
  • Mix of large, mid, and small-cap stocks
  • Never invest more than 5% in single stock
  • Rebalance annually

Peter Lynch - One Up On Wall Street

üîç The 6 Categories of Stocks

1. Slow Growers (Hold/Avoid)

Large, mature companies growing at 2-4% annually. Often pay dividends.

Example: Utilities, tobacco companies

2. Stalwarts (Buy on Dips)

Large companies growing 10-12% annually. Good for 30-50% gains.

Example: Coca-Cola, Johnson & Johnson

3. Fast Growers (Best Potential)

Small companies growing 20-25%+ annually. Lynch's favorites!

Caution: Can't sustain forever. Sell when growth slows.

4. Cyclicals (Timing Critical)

Sales/profits rise and fall with economic cycles.

Example: Airlines, auto manufacturers, steel

5. Turnarounds (High Risk/Reward)

Companies recovering from near-bankruptcy.

Key: No debt, good management, improving fundamentals

6. Asset Plays (Hidden Value)

Stock price doesn't reflect valuable assets.

Example: Real estate, patents, brand value

üéØ Lynch's 10 Baggers - Finding 10x Returns

  • "Invest in What You Know": Use your daily observations
  • The Perfect Stock: It sounds dull, does something dull, operates in a dull industry
  • Check the Fundamentals: Don't buy on story alone
  • P/E Ratio: Compare to growth rate (PEG < 1 is ideal)
  • Insider Buying: Good sign when execs buy their own stock
  • Look for Catalysts: New product, market expansion, cost-cutting
üìö

Warren Buffett Investment Philosophy

üè∞ Economic Moat

Sustainable Competitive Advantage

  • Brand Power: Coca-Cola, Apple - pricing power
  • Network Effects: Credit cards, social media
  • Cost Advantages: Economies of scale
  • High Switching Costs: Software, databases
  • Regulatory: Licenses, patents
Ask: Can competition easily replicate this business?

üìä Circle of Competence

Stay Within Your Expertise

  • Understand the business model completely
  • Know the industry dynamics
  • If you can't explain it simply, don't invest
  • It's okay to say "I don't know" and pass
Buffett Quote: "Risk comes from not knowing what you're doing"

üíµ Owner Earnings

Focus on Real Cash Generation

  • Net Income + Depreciation/Amortization
  • Minus: Maintenance Capital Expenditures
  • This is the TRUE cash the business generates
  • Compare to Market Cap to find undervalued stocks

"Our Favorite Holding Period is Forever"

  • Ignore short-term market noise
  • Buy businesses, not stocks
  • Let compound interest work its magic
  • Minimize taxes through long-term holdings
